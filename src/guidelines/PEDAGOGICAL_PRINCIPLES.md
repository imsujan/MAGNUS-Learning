## Core Educational Philosophy

### Our Learning Commitment

**AMD Platform Promise:**
> "Every learner who completes an AMD course will gain job-ready skills through hands-on practice, real-world projects, and industry-aligned instructionâ€”not just watch videos and take quizzes."

### Foundational Beliefs

1. **Skills Over Knowledge**
   - We teach what learners can DO, not just KNOW
   - Every course results in portfolio artifacts
   - Assessment measures performance, not recall

2. **Practice Over Lectures**
   - Learners spend majority of time building, creating, solving
   - Lectures are brief introductions to hands-on work
   - "Learning by doing" is mandatory, not optional

3. **Real-World Over Academic**
   - All content mirrors actual job tasks
   - Projects come from industry scenarios
   - Skills transfer immediately to workplace

4. **Inclusive Over Exclusive**
   - Courses accessible to learners of all backgrounds
   - Multiple pathways to success
   - Support for diverse learning needs

5. **Growth Over Fixed Mindset**
   - Mistakes are learning opportunities
   - Struggle is expected and supported
   - Mastery achieved through iteration

### 2. Learn â†’ Try â†’ Apply â†’ Assess â†’ Reflect Model

**Every module MUST follow this structure:**

#### **LEARN (10-20% of time)**
*Purpose: Acquire foundational knowledge*

**What it includes:**
- Brief video explanations (5-15 min)
- Demonstrations with clear narration
- Conceptual understanding
- "Why this matters" context

**What it does NOT include:**
- Hour-long lectures
- Passive content consumption
- Theory without application

**Quality Standard:** Learner should say, "I understand the concept and am ready to try it."

---

#### **TRY (20-30% of time)**
*Purpose: Guided practice in safe environment*

**What it includes:**
- Step-by-step exercises
- Instructor-provided templates/starters
- Low-stakes practice
- Immediate feedback mechanisms

**What it does NOT include:**
- Being thrown into deep end without scaffolding
- High-stakes assessments at this stage
- Solo struggle without guidance

**Quality Standard:** Learner should say, "I can follow the process with guidance."

---

#### **APPLY (40-50% of time)** â­ MOST IMPORTANT
*Purpose: Independent skill demonstration*

**What it includes:**
- Real-world projects
- Open-ended challenges
- Creative problem-solving
- Multiple solution pathways

**What it does NOT include:**
- Cookbook recipes with one answer
- Disconnected from reality
- Artificial academic exercises

**Quality Standard:** Learner should say, "I can use this skill independently in new contexts."

---

#### **ASSESS (10-15% of time)**
*Purpose: Verify learning and provide feedback*

**What it includes:**
- Formative assessments (quizzes, knowledge checks)
- Summative assessments (projects, exams)
- Rubric-based evaluation
- Constructive feedback

**What it does NOT include:**
- Trick questions
- Subjective grading without criteria
- Assessment without feedback
- "Gotcha" tests

**Quality Standard:** Learner should say, "I know what I've mastered and what needs work."

---

#### **REFLECT (5-10% of time)**
*Purpose: Metacognitive awareness and transfer*

**What it includes:**
- Self-assessment questions
- Reflection prompts
- Application planning ("How will I use this?")
- Identifying growth areas

**What it does NOT include:**
- Skipping reflection entirely
- Generic "What did you learn?" questions
- No connection to future application

**Quality Standard:** Learner should say, "I understand my learning process and next steps."

### 3. The 70-20-10 Learning Model

**MANDATORY distribution across all courses:**

**70% Experiential Learning (Hands-On Practice)**
- Building, creating, solving, designing
- Projects and labs
- Real-world application
- Skill practice and repetition

**Enforcement Metric:** 
> Count total course time. Minimum 70% must be spent on hands-on activities, projects, and practice exercises.

**How to Measure:**
- If course claims "40 hours," at minimum 28 hours must be active practice
- Video lectures should not exceed 10-12 hours (25-30%)
- Assessments and reflection can be 2-4 hours (5-10%)

---

**20% Social Learning (Collaboration)**
- Discussion forums participation
- Peer review activities
- Group projects (future feature)
- Community knowledge sharing

**Enforcement Metric:**
> Every module must include discussion prompts and/or peer review components.

---

**10% Formal Learning (Instruction)**
- Video lectures
- Reading materials
- Demonstrations
- Theoretical concepts

**Enforcement Metric:**
> Total instructional video time should be â‰¤ 25% of claimed course duration.

### 7. Portfolio-Driven Capstone Projects

**EVERY complete course must include a comprehensive capstone:**

**Capstone Characteristics:**

**Integrative:**
- Requires ALL major skills from the course
- Cannot be completed by mastering only part of content
- Tests synthesis and integration, not isolated skills

**Realistic:**
- Based on actual professional deliverables
- Uses authentic project scenarios
- Includes realistic constraints and requirements
- Mirrors job responsibilities

**Rigorous:**
- Requires significant time investment (5-20% of total course time)
- Challenges learners to demonstrate mastery
- Has clear quality standards
- Differentiates levels of achievement

**Portfolio-Worthy:**
- Professional presentation quality
- Demonstrates competency to employers
- Can be showcased on LinkedIn, personal websites
- Includes documentation explaining process and decisions

**Assessment:**
- Detailed rubric with 4-6 criteria
- Milestones with formative feedback
- Final evaluation with constructive critique
- Option for revision based on feedback

**Example Capstone Projects:**

- **BIM Course:** Model a complete multi-story building with coordinated MEP, clash detection, and construction document set
- **Revit Families Course:** Create a complex parametric family library (10+ families) with documentation
- **AutoCAD Course:** Produce a full construction drawing package for a commercial building
- **MEPF Course:** Design and document complete HVAC/plumbing/electrical systems for a building

**Minimum Capstone Standards:**
- 5-10 hours of work required
- Professional quality output
- Comprehensive rubric (4+ criteria, 4-level scale)
- Feedback at milestones
- Reflection component required

## Platform-Wide Standards

### Accessibility Requirements (Non-Negotiable)

**ALL content MUST meet these accessibility standards:**

**Video:**
- [ ] Accurate captions (human-reviewed, not auto-generated only)
- [ ] Downloadable transcripts provided
- [ ] No flashing content (3Hz+ flashing can trigger seizures)
- [ ] Audio descriptions or narration of visual-only content

**Visual Design:**
- [ ] Sufficient color contrast (WCAG AA: 4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for large text)
- [ ] Information not conveyed by color alone
- [ ] Alt text for all images
- [ ] Readable font sizes (14pt minimum in documents)

**Navigation:**
- [ ] Keyboard accessible (no mouse-only interactions)
- [ ] Screen reader compatible
- [ ] Logical heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3)
- [ ] Skip navigation links

**Content:**
- [ ] Plain language (8th-10th grade reading level for general audiences)
- [ ] Technical jargon defined
- [ ] Short sentences (15-20 words average)
- [ ] Active voice preferred

**Compliance:**
- WCAG 2.1 Level AA minimum
- Section 508 compliance (US)
- ADA compliance

**Tools for Verification:**
- WebAIM Contrast Checker (color contrast)
- Hemingway Editor (reading level)
- Screen reader testing (NVDA, JAWS)
- Accessibility audit before publication

---

### Technical Quality Baselines

**ALL courses must meet these technical standards:**

**Video Quality:**
- Resolution: 1080p minimum (1920x1080)
- Frame rate: 30fps minimum
- File format: MP4 (H.264 codec)
- No visual artifacts (pixelation, compression issues)

**Audio Quality:**
- Clear, intelligible speech
- Consistent volume levels (-12dB to -6dB average)
- No background noise (hiss, hum, echo)
- Professional microphone (not laptop built-in)
- No audio clipping

**Editing:**
- Tight editing (no long pauses, dead air, excessive "umms")
- Professional intro/outro cards
- On-screen text overlays readable
- Cursor movements deliberate (not erratic)
- Zoom-ins for small UI elements

**Load Times:**
- Videos compress appropriately (quality vs file size balance)
- Pages load quickly (<3 seconds)
- Images optimized (WebP or compressed JPG/PNG)

**Rejection Criteria:**
- Video resolution below 720p
- Audio quality poor (background noise, low volume, distortion)
- Amateur editing (confusing cuts, no structure)

---

### Global Inclusivity Standards

**ALL courses must be globally accessible:**

**Language:**
- Use clear, simple English
- Avoid idioms, slang, cultural references
- Define acronyms and abbreviations
- Consistent terminology (don't alternate synonyms)

**Examples & Scenarios:**
- Include diverse names (not all Western)
- International examples (not US-centric only)
- Neutral pronouns (they/them or "the learner")
- No assumptions about holidays, schedules, family structures

**Practical Considerations:**
- Multiple time zones respected (asynchronous preferred)
- Low-bandwidth options (video quality settings)
- Downloadable content for offline use
- Mobile-responsive design

**Cultural Sensitivity:**
- Respectful of all cultures
- No stereotypes or bias
- Inclusive imagery and examples
- Global perspective on industry practices
