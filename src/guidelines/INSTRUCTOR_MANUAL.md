## Course Design Philosophy

### The AMD Learning Framework

Every course on our platform follows this proven structure:

**LEARN â†’ TRY â†’ APPLY â†’ ASSESS â†’ REFLECT**

Let's break this down:

#### **1. LEARN** (10-20% of time)
- Watch video or read content
- Understand concepts and theory
- See demonstrations
- Review examples

**Your Role:** Explain clearly, show real examples, tell stories

#### **2. TRY** (20-30% of time)
- Guided practice exercises
- Follow-along tutorials
- Low-stakes experimentation
- Immediate feedback

**Your Role:** Provide step-by-step instructions, create safe practice space

#### **3. APPLY** (40-50% of time)
- Independent projects
- Real-world scenarios
- Problem-solving challenges
- Creative application

**Your Role:** Design realistic projects, provide frameworks, guide troubleshooting

#### **4. ASSESS** (10-15% of time)
- Quizzes to test understanding
- Project evaluations
- Self-assessments
- Peer reviews

**Your Role:** Create fair assessments, provide constructive feedback, set clear criteria

#### **5. REFLECT** (5-10% of time)
- What did I learn?
- How will I use this?
- What challenged me?
- What's next?

**Your Role:** Ask thought-provoking questions, encourage metacognition

- Discussion forums

- **Specific:** Clear and precise
- **Measurable:** Observable and testable
- **Achievable:** Realistic for skill level
- **Relevant:** Directly applicable to jobs/goals
- **Time-bound:** Achievable within course duration

## Writing Learning Objectives

### Bloom's Taxonomy in Practice

Learning objectives describe what learners will be able to DO after completing a lesson/module.

**Bloom's Taxonomy Levels (Revised):**

```
Level 1: REMEMBER (Recall facts)
Level 2: UNDERSTAND (Explain concepts)
Level 3: APPLY (Use in new situations)
Level 4: ANALYZE (Break down and examine)
Level 5: EVALUATE (Judge and critique)
Level 6: CREATE (Design and build)
```

**For job-ready skills, focus on Levels 3-6 (Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, Create).**

### Action Verbs by Level

**Level 1: REMEMBER**
- Define, List, Name, Identify, Recall, Label, Match, Select

**Level 2: UNDERSTAND**
- Explain, Describe, Summarize, Paraphrase, Interpret, Classify, Compare

**Level 3: APPLY** â­ Start here for practical courses
- Use, Demonstrate, Execute, Implement, Operate, Solve, Calculate, Perform

**Level 4: ANALYZE** â­
- Examine, Investigate, Differentiate, Organize, Deconstruct, Diagnose, Test

**Level 5: EVALUATE** â­
- Assess, Critique, Judge, Justify, Validate, Verify, Recommend, Prioritize

**Level 6: CREATE** â­ Ultimate goal
- Design, Develop, Build, Construct, Plan, Produce, Compose, Generate

### Writing Effective Learning Objectives

**Formula:**
> By the end of this [lesson/module], you will be able to **[Action Verb]** + **[What]** + **[Context/How]** + **[Quality Criteria (optional)]**.

**Examples:**

**For a Revit Lesson:**
> "By the end of this lesson, you will be able to **create** **custom wall types** in Revit with multiple layers and assign material properties."

**For an AutoCAD Module:**
> "By the end of this module, you will be able to **produce** **complete construction drawings** including title blocks, viewports, and annotations that meet industry standards."

**For a Navisworks Lesson:**
> "By the end of this lesson, you will be able to **identify and categorize** **clash conflicts** between architectural and MEP models using Clash Detective."

## Designing Hands-On Labs

### Why Hands-On Practice Matters

**The Learning Pyramid (retention rates):**
- Lecture: 5% retention
- Reading: 10% retention
- Audiovisual: 20% retention
- Demonstration: 30% retention
- **Practice by doing: 75% retention** â­
- **Teaching others: 90% retention** â­

**Your job: Get learners DOING, not just watching.**

### Types of Practice Activities

**1. Guided Practice (Follow-Along)**
- Instructor demonstrates step-by-step
- Learner pauses video and replicates
- Low complexity, high support
- Builds confidence

**Example:** "Follow along as I model this simple chair in Revit. Pause the video after each step and replicate on your own."

---

**2. Scaffolded Practice (Partially Complete)**
- Instructor provides starter files
- Learner completes missing portions
- Medium complexity, medium support
- Develops problem-solving

**Example:** "I've provided a partially complete Revit model. Your task: Add the missing windows and doors according to the floor plan provided."

---

**3. Independent Practice (From Scratch)**
- Learner builds entire solution
- Instructor provides requirements only
- High complexity, low support (but available)
- Tests true understanding

**Example:** "Create a complete staircase family with the following specifications: [list requirements]. Use the techniques from this module."

---

**4. Challenge Practice (Stretch Goals)**
- Advanced optional tasks
- Requires creative thinking
- No step-by-step instructions
- For motivated learners

**Example:** "Advanced Challenge: Can you modify this family to include automatic railing generation based on stair geometry? Hint: nested families."

## Building Quizzes & Assessments

### Assessment Philosophy

**Two types of assessments:**

**1. Formative Assessments** (Learning checkpoints)
- **Purpose:** Check understanding, provide feedback
- **Stakes:** Low (can retake unlimited times)
- **Placement:** After each lesson or module
- **Types:** Quick quizzes, knowledge checks
- **Feedback:** Immediate, with explanations

**2. Summative Assessments** (Prove mastery)
- **Purpose:** Evaluate overall competency
- **Stakes:** Medium (limited attempts)
- **Placement:** End of modules or course
- **Types:** Projects, comprehensive exams
- **Feedback:** Detailed, with improvement guidance

### Writing Effective Quiz Questions

#### **Multiple Choice Questions**

**Anatomy of a good MCQ:**

```
Question Stem: [Clear, specific question]

A) [Plausible distractor]
B) [Correct answer]
C) [Common misconception]
D) [Plausible distractor]

Explanation: [Why B is correct and why others are wrong]
```

**Example:**

**Question:** In Revit, which parameter type should you use if you want the value to be the same for all instances of a family type, but different between types?

A) Instance Parameter  
B) Type Parameter âœ…  
C) Shared Parameter  
D) Global Parameter  

**Explanation:** Type Parameters are the same for all instances of a specific type, but can differ between types (e.g., all "36x80 Doors" have the same height, but "30x80 Doors" have a different height). Instance Parameters vary for each individual element. Shared Parameters are for scheduling, and Global Parameters control multiple families.

---

**MCQ Best Practices:**

âœ… **Do:**
- Test understanding, not memorization
- Use real-world scenarios
- Make all options plausible
- Avoid "all of the above" or "none of the above"
- Keep options similar in length
- Use clear, concise language

âŒ **Don't:**
- Use trick questions
- Include obvious wrong answers
- Use double negatives
- Give hints through grammar (a/an inconsistencies)
- Make correct answer always "B" or "C"

---

#### **True/False Questions**

**Best for:** Testing specific facts or identifying misconceptions

**Example:**

**Question:** True or False: In AutoCAD, layers can be used to control both the visibility and the plotting of objects.

**Answer:** True âœ…

**Explanation:** Layers in AutoCAD control multiple properties including visibility (on/off, freeze/thaw), plotting (plot/no plot), color, and linetype.

---

#### **Fill-in-the-Blank Questions**

**Best for:** Testing recall of terminology or specific values

**Example:**

**Question:** In Revit, the command to create a new family from scratch is found in the __________ menu.

**Answer:** File â†’ New â†’ Family (or "File" is acceptable)

**Grading:** Use flexible matching (accept variations, ignore capitalization)

---

#### **Matching Questions**

**Best for:** Testing understanding of relationships or categories

**Example:**

1. Door Family          â†’ A. Structural framing
2. Generic Model        â†’ B. Opening in a wall
3. Structural Framing   â†’ C. Custom non-standard geometry

#### **Scenario-Based Questions (Applied Knowledge)**

**Best for:** Testing ability to apply knowledge to real situations

**Example:**

**Scenario:** You're working on a BIM coordination project and notice that HVAC ducts are clashing with structural beams in 15 locations. The structural engineer says the beams cannot be moved. What is the BEST next step?

A) Move the ducts to avoid the beams and update the MEP model

**2. Provide Multiple Explanations**

- Visual + verbal + text

**3. Include Summaries and Repetition**
